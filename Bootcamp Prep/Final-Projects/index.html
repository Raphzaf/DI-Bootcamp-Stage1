Tribute to Edward Newgate - Whitebeard

"The strongest man in the world, and the most compassionate."

Biography

Edward Newgate, best known as Whitebeard, was the captain of the Whitebeard Pirates and one of the Four Emperors. He was considered the strongest man in the world after Gol D. Roger's death and ruled the seas for decades. Born on Sphinx Island, he never sought glory or power — only a family.

A Father to His Crew

What made Whitebeard truly unique wasn't just his overwhelming strength, but his heart. He saw every one of his crewmates as his sons. That’s why they all called him “Father.” He protected, guided, and loved them as a true family. In a world of pirates, that made him a legend.

Timeline of His Life

  • Childhood: Orphaned on Sphinx Island, he dreamed of having a family.
  • Joined the Rocks Pirates: His first steps into piracy, alongside legends.
  • Formed his own crew: Founder of the Whitebeard Pirates.
  • Clashes with Roger: Titanic battles, mutual respect between kings.
  • Adopted Ace: He accepted Gol D. Roger’s son as his own.
  • Paramount War: Invaded Marineford to save Ace — the ultimate act of loyalty.
  • Death: Died standing, his body covered in wounds, never turning his back on the enemy.

A Legendary Farewell

Whitebeard died standing tall, chest bare, eyes fixed on his enemies. No wounds on his back — he never ran. His ultimate sacrifice shook the world. Even in death, he protected his legacy and his “sons.”
